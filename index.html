The Hard Truth

You're not running a boutique property.
You're running one for Airbnb.

01

Your guests aren't yours.

Every booking made through a platform belongs to the platform. The name, the email, the phone number — all locked behind a wall you don't control. The day they change their algorithm, your calendar empties.

02

You're paying for your own competition.

The 15–20% you hand over each night funds the very platform that shows your guests three cheaper alternatives. You are financing the system that undercuts you — every single booking.

03

Reviews are a trap, not an asset.

Five years of five-star reviews live on someone else's server. You can't export them, migrate them, or survive without them. You've built a reputation you don't own, on land you don't rent.

04

Busy doesn't mean profitable.

High occupancy on a platform calendar is vanity. What matters is margin — and margin disappears when a third party sits between every guest and every pound, euro, or dollar you earn.

None of this is your fault. The platforms were designed this way.
But staying is a choice — and there's a way out.
